lag-lock uncertainty

Field: Climate Economics | JEL Codes: D81, E24, J64, Q54

Definition

The compounding uncertainty arising from temporal lags between climate policy actions and observable climate system responses, where committed future warming from past emissions (climate inertia) combines with uncertainty about threshold locations and feedback strengths to create decision-making paralysis and difficulty in attributing outcomes to specific interventions.

Etymology

Compound construction combining three elements:.

Usage Example

Lag-lock uncertainty complicates climate negotiations, as policymakers cannot observe whether current mitigation efforts are sufficient until decades after emissions peak, by which time irreversible tipping points may have been crossed". royalsocietypublishing.
